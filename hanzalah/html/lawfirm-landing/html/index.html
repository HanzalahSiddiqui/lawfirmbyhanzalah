How can I get custody of my child in Pakistan?

Child Protection Courts will have authority to hand over custody of a child to parents, suitable person or child protection institution as the case may be but preference will be given to parents and extended families. The court shall inform the child of the situation and will take its views before making decision.

How can I divorce my wife/husband in Pakistan?

1. Marriage can be dissolved by husband at his will without the intervention of a court commonly called a “Talaq”
2. Wife can also seeks dissolution of marriage by way of applying “Khula” through the court.
3. By mutual consent of the husband and wife, without intervention of the court;

Who has the right of custody over a child?

Parents of the minor child shall have the right to custody over their children. The right of custody accorded to parents springs from the exercise of parental authority. Under the Family Code, the father and the mother shall jointly exercise parental authority over the persons of their common children.

Can child stay with father after divorce?

The mother has a right of custody even if she is divorced but in case of remarriage after divorce and when the child get adult, the custody belongs to the father.

Can wife get maintenance without divorce in Pakistan?

According to wife maintenance law in Pakistan, the wife is entitled to maintenance however there is no wife maintenance after divorce in Pakistan as after divorce she ceases to be wife. Wife maintenance a month depends on the financial condition of the husband which the court has to decide.

How long does a divorce take in Pakistan?

Finally, initiate proceedings according to divorce laws in Pakistan. After initiation of proceedings and completing the procedure of divorce certificate. The union Council takes 90 to 120 days to issue divorce certificate.

What happens if husband does not pay maintenance in Pakistan?

According to section 5 of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act 1964, a Judge of a Family Court could also exercise his powers to pass the judgment and decree. Orders of maintenance could be passed against defaulting husbands and fathers by using criminal as well as civil remedies.
